Reference

Compensation formulas

Primary disorderExpected compensation used
Metabolic acidosisExpected PaCO₂ = 1.5 × HCO₃⁻ + 8 ± 2
Metabolic alkalosisExpected PaCO₂ = 40 + 0.7 × (HCO₃⁻ − 24) ± 2; compensation generally does not raise PaCO₂ beyond ~55 mm Hg
Acute respiratory acidosisHCO₃⁻ rises ~1 mEq/L per 10-mm Hg PaCO₂ increase
Chronic respiratory acidosisHCO₃⁻ rises ~3.5–4 mEq/L per 10-mm Hg PaCO₂ increase
Acute respiratory alkalosisHCO₃⁻ falls ~2 mEq/L per 10-mm Hg PaCO₂ decrease
Chronic respiratory alkalosisHCO₃⁻ falls ~4–5 mEq/L per 10-mm Hg PaCO₂ decrease
Anion gapAG = Na⁺ − (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻); corrected AG = AG + 2.5 × (4 − albumin)
Delta ratio(Corrected AG − 12) / (24 − HCO₃⁻). Values <1 suggest additional normal-gap acidosis; >2 suggest additional metabolic alkalosis.
Internal consistencyCalculated pH = 6.1 + log₁₀[HCO₃⁻ / (0.03 × PaCO₂)]

Normal values and compensation slopes vary slightly by source and laboratory. The app uses common bedside approximations and displays uncertainty rather than treating them as exact physiologic laws.
