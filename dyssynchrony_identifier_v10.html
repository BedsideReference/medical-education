      This is a teaching tool. The pressure and flow tracings, the severity labels, and the management lists are idealized, schematic representations of each dyssynchrony pattern generated from simple equations — they are not patient data and not the output of any real ventilator. It does not constitute medical advice and is not a substitute for clinical judgment, hands-on training on the actual device, institutional protocols, or the care of a qualified clinician at the bedside.

      Real waveforms vary with patient effort, lung mechanics, mode, ventilator manufacturer, and circuit factors; dyssynchronies frequently coexist, appear atypically, or mimic one another, and a single tracing rarely identifies a pattern with certainty. The corrective actions shown are general teaching points, not a protocol — the right intervention depends on the individual patient and context. Use this to build pattern-recognition intuition, never to make, guide, or justify a patient-care decision.

      Verify anything clinically actionable against primary sources, current society guidelines, and your institution's standards before applying it to a patient.
